I am a computer science Ph.D. student at North Carolina State University working with Dr. Kristy Boyer. My current research goal is to apply machine learning to build dialogue systems that improve as they interact with users. For my master's thesis, I extended a probabilistic model of topics in documents called Latent Dirichlet Allocation in order to automatically discover how the words associated with a topic (e.g., legal cases) depend on time (e.g., the name of a company involved in an ongoing case).

Other interests include web deveopment, mobile app development, and coffee.

Masters Research

Legal topic table. This is a table from my master's thesis showing the words in a set of news articles and blog posts related to "legal cases" in each of several weeks.

Gibbs sampling animation. This is an animation of collapsed Gibbs sampling for Latent Dirichlet Allocation. Documents 0-40 are online reviews for a farmer's market, and 41-81 are online reviews for a Thai restaurant. Common words and punctuation have been removed. There are 2 topics, indicated by green and orange. The assignment of topics to words is initially random but quickly converges so that each topic corresponds to one of the review categories.
